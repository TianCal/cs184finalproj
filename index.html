Final Proj Proposal

Title: Shaders, Shaders, Shaders, and Shaders

Team Members: Fanjia Yan, Jason Wang, Yuqi Hu, Li Tian

Summary:

We will build 4 shader programs with ShaderToy to render creative scenes. They include volanic eruption, realistic terrain without textures, cartoon rendering with tonal shading, and seaweed movement rendering.

Problem Description:

We love all different shaders that seem impossible with only a hundred lines of code. We want to build 4 creative shaders with ShaderToy. Each one will generally render a different scene, including volanic eruption, realistic terrain without textures, cartoon rendering with tonal shading, and seaweed movement rendering.

Goals & Deliverables

Our idea stems from the implementation of Phong shading in Project 2 and a variety of shadings for Cloth in Project 4. We, as a team wants to creatively explore more complex and realistic shaders for renderings. We will rely on our creativity as well as recent developments in computer graphics research to derive our shading mode. Our goal is to build 4 different shader programs via ShaderToy. The details are listed below:

1. Volcanic eruption rendering

  • Plan: We plan to build a shader program that renders the scene of volcanic eruption. Most shaders on ShaderToy process eruption like a flame on the crater. We hope to render the magma as fluids to present a better rendering.
  • Hope: If our plan goes out successfully, we might be able to combine the rendering of flames and fluids in eruption to make it even more realistic.

2. Realistic Terrain Rendering without textures

  • Plan: Recent 3D games are becoming larger and larger in size, some even reached the size of 100GB. Part of the reason I think is because of the high-resolution textures stored in the game. In this part, we will build a shader program that renders realistic terrain with little or no textures at all, shrinking the size to several KB while still producing realistic visuals. There will be landscapes, trees, clouds, and some lighting/shading. We will start with a fixed camera, and will do moving camera after the render looks good.
  • Hope: If things go smoothly, we will probably also build new features such as simulating climate/time/season changes.

3. Cartoon Rendering with tonal shading:

  • Plan: the deliverable of this shader program will be a completed shader program that will take in texture map and model and output cartoonish object.
  • Hope: What I hope to achieve for this shader is that for any shape of object, the shader program is able to patch the object with limited amount of texture map and create cartoon style animated outer surface.

4. Rendering Seaweed with Wind without textures

  • Plan: The plan is to render the seaweed waving under the water while a camera is in the water. In the process, to differentiate from rendering water flow, the camera will be assumed to be down towards the water, so no need to consider the rendering of water (also saves rendering time). Also, to imitate the underwater environment, stickers like fishes or shrimps will be added to the background. The hardest point would be dealing with the narrow end of the seaweeds.
  • Hope: If everything goes well, the first thing to add up with is the ocean flow so that the water flow and the sense of dense and texture of water can better restore the real case. Moreover, the fishes and shrimps can also be rendered with specific textures. At last, we would call this one the hope of the hope which is to make a scene where a volcano eruption happens in a realistic terrain which is right near shallow water where seaweeds in it and all the objects has tonal shading effects.
